    Types of roblox games> (Also known as genres):

    • Roblox features a wide variety of game genres, each offering unique gameplay experiences. Here are the main genres and their explanations:
    • NOTE: There are about 16 different types of genres on roblox

    line spacing

    (Obby (Obstacle Course))

    -Obbies are parkour-style games where players must navigate through challenging obstacle courses. These games test agility, precision, and patience as players jump over gaps, avoid traps, and complete levels.

    (Simulator)

    -In simulator games, players engage in repetitive activities (such as clicking, collecting, or training) to gain in-game currency or strength. These games often involve upgrading abilities, rebirths, and progression mechanics, making them highly addictive.

    (Roleplay (RP))

    -Tycoon games revolve around managing and building businesses, factories, or empires. Players generate income, unlock new features, and expand their operations, usually in an idle or incremental gameplay style.

    (Tycoon)

    -Tycoon games revolve around managing and building businesses, factories, or empires. Players generate income, unlock new features, and expand their operations, usually in an idle or incremental gameplay style.

    (Fighting)

    -This genre includes player-vs-player (PvP) combat games where users fight using melee weapons, guns, or special abilities. Games like "Mortal Kombat" or "Street Fighter"-style brawlers fall into this category.

    (Shooter (FPS/TPS)) (My personal favorite).

    -Shooter games involve first-person or third-person gunplay. These games range from military-style battles to casual, fun-oriented shooters like "Arsenal" and "Big Paintball!"

    (Survival)

    -Players must gather resources, craft items, and survive in harsh environments. Some survival games feature zombies natural disasters, or battle royale-style mechanics.

    (Horror)

    -Horror games on Roblox create spooky and suspenseful experiences. Some focus on escaping terrifying creatures, solving puzzles, or experiencing eerie storytelling. Popular examples include "Doors" and "The Mimic."

    (Adventure/Exploration)

    -These games emphasize open-world exploration, quests, and discovery. Players often embark on journeys, solve puzzles, and interact with various NPCs and environments.

    (Building & Sandbox)

    -This genre allows players to create their own worlds using tools and assets. Games like "Welcome to Bloxburg" and "Build A Boat For Treasure" give players creative freedom to design structures and objects.

    (Racing)

    -Racing games feature cars, motorcycles, boats, or other vehicles competing in fast-paced tracks. Some include stunt mechanics drifting, and customization.

    (Sports)

    -Sports games replicate real-world sports like soccer basketball, and football. Some focus on realistic mechanics, while others take a more arcade-style approach.

    (Anime/Fighting RPGs)

    Inspired by anime series, these games allow players to train, fight, and unlock special abilities based on anime universes. Popular ones include "Blox Fruits" and "Shindo Life."

    (Music & Rhythm)

    -Music-based games challenge players to follow beats and rhythms, often involving dancing, tapping buttons in sync , or DJ-style mixing.

    (Clicker/Incremental)

    -These games involve clicking to earn points and progress through upgrades. They often feature "rebirth" systems to start over with better rewards.

    (Story-Based)

    -Story-driven games take players on a scripted journey with dialogue, cutscenes, and choices that impact the narrative.
